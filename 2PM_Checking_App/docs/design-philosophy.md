# Bob — User-Centric Design Philosophy

**Plain and simple: we design for the person on the site, not for the system.**

---

- **One job per screen.** Each screen answers one question or does one thing. No cramming. Foremen should know in one glance what to do.

- **Status first.** Ready or not ready, open or closed—the answer is always visible and obvious. No hunting. Use big labels and clear colors so anyone can tell at a glance.

- **Big taps, few steps.** Buttons are large and easy to hit, even with gloves or in sun. Primary action (e.g. confirm readiness, report issue) is the biggest and clearest. Secondary actions (filters, settings) are still tappable but visually secondary.

- **Readable in any condition.** Text is large and simple. We use a small set of sizes (title, body, caption) and a highly legible sans-serif. Works in bright sun, dust, and quick glances.

- **Photos and notes do the talking.** When reporting issues, the photo is big and the note is short. We don’t ask for essays—we ask for “what’s wrong” in a few words and one clear image.

- **Consistent, not clever.** Same header pattern, same button style, same card style everywhere. New screens copy the patterns we already use so the app feels familiar and predictable.

- **Friendly, not corporate.** The app feels approachable and a bit gamified (clear wins, clear status). It’s built for real people on noisy job sites, not for office paperwork.

---

*Summary: Clarity over complexity. Glanceable status. Big taps. Simple words. One thing per screen. We only use our shared components and standard layouts.*
